PlanTube · User Manual · v1.0

User Manual

Everything you need to import playlists, build a study schedule, use AI tutoring, host watch parties, and track your learning progress — all in one place.

Introduction

PlanTube turns passive YouTube watching into active, structured learning. You import any YouTube playlist, schedule its videos using a spaced repetition algorithm, and study them inside a distraction-free Focus Mode packed with AI tools, notes, and collaboration features.

Progress is gamified with XP, daily streaks, and achievements. You can study alongside friends in synchronized watch parties, form study groups to share progress on playlists, and compete on a global leaderboard.

Focus Mode
Distraction-free player with custom controls, chapters, notes, AI chat, and every study tool in one view.
Spaced Repetition
SM-2 algorithm (same as Anki) calculates optimal review dates so you revisit videos at exactly the right time.
AI Tutoring
Chat, flashcards, quizzes, and brainstorming — all using the current video's transcript as context.
Watch Party
Host a synchronized viewing session. Guests join with a code and are automatically taken to the right video.
Gamification
Earn XP, maintain daily streaks, unlock 15+ achievements, and climb the leaderboard.
Library & Analytics
Activity heatmap, watch history, completion rates, and a full library of every video you've touched.

Quick start

You can be watching your first scheduled video in under three minutes:

1
Create an accountSign up with an email and password. Your progress, notes, and settings are tied to your account and sync across devices.
2
Import a playlistGo to Import in the nav. Paste any public YouTube playlist URL. PlanTube fetches all video metadata via the YouTube Data API.
3
Open Focus ModeClick any video thumbnail from your Dashboard or playlist page to open the distraction-free player.
4
Mark it completeWhen you're done watching, press C or click the checkmark. Rate it Easy / Medium / Hard. PlanTube schedules your next review automatically.
5
Come back tomorrowYour Dashboard shows exactly which videos are due today based on your spaced repetition schedule. Study consistently and your streaks build.
Tip
You can also paste a single YouTube video URL into Import — PlanTube creates a single-video playlist for it. Great for one-off lectures or tutorials.

Importing playlists

PlanTube supports any public or unlisted YouTube playlist URL. Private playlists cannot be imported — YouTube's API does not expose them to third-party apps.

During import, PlanTube stores the playlist metadata (title, thumbnail, video count) and fetches each video's title, duration, and thumbnail from YouTube. This data is deduplicated — if two users import the same video, only one copy of the metadata is stored on the server.

Note
Import time depends on playlist length. A 10-video playlist imports in about 2 seconds. A 200-video course may take 10–15 seconds. The page shows a progress indicator while fetching.

After importing

The imported playlist appears on your Dashboard and in My Playlists. From the playlist page you can:

  • Watch any video in Focus Mode by clicking its thumbnail.
  • Schedule the entire playlist with one click — PlanTube spreads videos across days using SM-2.
  • Share the playlist with a group or make it public for other users to discover.
  • Sync — re-fetch the playlist from YouTube to pick up newly added videos.

Focus Mode

Focus Mode is the core of PlanTube. It opens when you click any video and gives you a distraction-free player with every study tool within reach.

Controls & layout

The screen is divided into three areas:

  • Left rail — chapter list, playlist queue, AI tools, resources.
  • Player — the YouTube embed with custom controls underneath.
  • Right sidebar — notes, flashcards, watch party panel.

The top bar shows the video title, a progress indicator across the playlist, and quick-access buttons for mark-complete, fullscreen, and the top-bar menu.

Playback persistence

PlanTube remembers your volume level and playback speed across sessions. Set them once — they apply every time you open Focus Mode, even after closing the browser.

Your watch position is also saved every 10 seconds while playing. If you close a video mid-way and return later, you resume from where you left off.

Focus Lock

Press L to enable Focus Lock. This prevents accidental navigation away from the current video by intercepting browser back/forward and tab-close events. A padlock icon appears in the top bar while active. Useful if you're easily distracted by other browser tabs.

Zen mode

Press H or click "Hide UI" to remove all overlays and watch in pure fullscreen. Move the mouse to bring controls back. Press H again or move away to re-hide.

Mobile controls

On phones and tablets, a full horizontal row of 13+ icons would be unreadable. Instead, PlanTube shows 4 compact action buttons at the bottom right. Tap ⋯ to open a bottom sheet with all remaining tools arranged in a 4-column grid: Notes, Chapters, Playlist, Watch Party, AI, Flashcards, Resources, About, YouTube, Hide UI.

This keeps the player clean on small screens while keeping every tool one tap away.

Scheduling

PlanTube uses the SM-2 spaced repetition algorithm — the same algorithm used by Anki, SuperMemo, and most modern flashcard apps — to calculate optimal review dates for videos.

SM-2 algorithm

After you finish a video, you rate it:

RatingWhat it meansEffect
EasyFully understood, no effortLong interval — you won't see it for weeks
MediumUnderstood with some effortMedium interval — review in a few days
HardStruggled or partially understoodShort interval — back in 1–2 days

Each rating updates the video's easiness factor (EF) — a multiplier that adjusts the next interval. Easy ratings increase the EF (future gaps grow faster), hard ratings decrease it (you see the video more often until mastery is demonstrated).

The first time you complete a video, the interval is always short (1 day for Hard, 4 for Medium, 7 for Easy). After two or more completions, SM-2 uses the EF and previous interval to project further.

Calendar & due dates

The Dashboard calendar shows all scheduled videos per day. Each cell shows a count of due items. Click a day to see which videos are due. Overdue items appear in red.

You can also manually schedule any video to any date by right-clicking its card and selecting "Schedule for…". This overrides the SM-2 calculation for one cycle — the next review after manual completion still uses SM-2.

Tip
Rate honestly. Marking everything "Easy" defeats the purpose — you'll see hard videos too rarely and forget them. Rate "Hard" whenever you had to rewatch a section or still feel uncertain.

Notes

The notes panel in Focus Mode's right sidebar lets you capture thoughts without leaving the video.

Timestamped notes

Click the timestamp badge (showing the current playback position) before typing to pin a note to that moment. The saved note shows a clickable timestamp — click it later to jump back to exactly that point in the video.

Notes are saved automatically as you type. They're stored on the server and available across devices.

Formatting

  • Use ``` code ``` for inline code blocks.
  • Bullet lists with a dash at the start of a line.
  • Plain text for everything else — notes aren't meant to be a full editor.

Exporting

Click the copy icon in the notes panel header to copy all notes for the current video as Markdown. Paste into Obsidian, Notion, or any note-taking app that accepts Markdown.

Library & heatmap

The Library page shows every video you've interacted with — watched, scheduled, bookmarked, or completed — with filtering by status, playlist, and date range.

Activity heatmap

A GitHub-style contribution graph shows your daily watch activity for the past year. Each square is one day. Darker color = more videos watched that day.

Use this to spot your streaks, find days you fell off, and motivate consistency. A long horizontal run of dark squares is your best progress indicator.

Continue Watching

The Dashboard shows a "Continue Watching" row of videos you started but haven't finished, sorted by most recent. Click any card to return to Focus Mode — your position is restored automatically.

AI Tools

All AI tools in PlanTube use the current video's transcript as context. Every answer is specific to what's actually in the video — not generic internet knowledge.

Note
AI tools require the video to have auto-generated captions available on YouTube. Videos without any captions (some foreign-language or manually-uploaded videos) cannot use AI features. A message will appear in the panel if no transcript is available.

AI Chat

Open the AI tab in the left rail of Focus Mode. Type any question and receive an answer grounded in the video's transcript. Good prompts to try:

  • "Explain the concept at 5:30 like I'm a beginner."
  • "What are the three main takeaways from this video?"
  • "Give me two real-world examples of what was just explained."
  • "What questions would a professor ask about this content?"

The conversation is preserved per video — you can close the panel and come back to it later.

Flashcards

Click Generate Flashcards in the AI tab. PlanTube creates a deck of Q&A cards from the video content. Cards use a flip animation — see the question, think of the answer, then flip to check.

You can regenerate the deck at any time for a fresh set of questions.

Quiz

Generate a multiple-choice quiz about the current video. Each question shows 4 options. Selecting a wrong answer reveals an explanation pulled from the transcript. A score summary appears when you complete all questions.

Brainstorm

Open-ended idea generation based on the video's topic. Useful for: project ideas inspired by the content, essay outlines, study questions to test yourself, or follow-up research directions. The output is a bulleted list you can copy.

Watch Party

Watch Party lets you watch a YouTube video in real-time sync with friends. The host controls playback — play, pause, and seek events are broadcast to all guests instantly.

Hosting a party

1
Open a videoNavigate to any video in Focus Mode. The video you're currently watching becomes the party's video.
2
Open Watch PartyClick the Users icon in the sidebar or in the ⋯ sheet on mobile. The Watch Party panel opens.
3
Create RoomClick Create Room (Host). A 6-character room code is generated instantly.
4
Share the invite linkClick Copy invite link to copy a URL your friends can paste directly into their browser — no manual code needed.
5
Use Sync Play / Sync PauseOnly the host controls playback. Use the Sync Play and Sync Pause buttons in the panel to broadcast your position to all guests.

Joining a party

Guests no longer need to find the right video manually. Here's how it works:

1
Open Watch Party panelGo to any page in Focus Mode (or the homepage). Open the Watch Party panel — it doesn't matter which video you're on.
2
Enter the room codeType the 6-character code from the host (or click the invite link). A preview card appears showing the video thumbnail, title, and number of people watching.
3
Click Go & JoinYou're automatically navigated to the correct video and joined to the party. No manual searching required.
Tip
If you're already on the same video as the party, clicking Join Party joins instantly without any navigation.

Reactions

Use the emoji reaction buttons (👍 🎉 🔥 💡 😮 ❤️) to send a reaction to everyone in the party. Reactions float up on all participants' screens in real time.

Note
The watch party room exists only while the host has Focus Mode open. If the host closes their browser, the room is destroyed and guests are disconnected. The host must create a new room to restart.

Study Groups

Study Groups let you share playlists with friends and see each other's progress video-by-video.

Creating a group

Go to Groups in the nav. Click "Create Group", set a name and description. You become the group admin. Share the invite code or link with your friends — they can join from the Groups page.

Group playlists

Add any of your playlists to the group from the group page. Once added, all members can view the playlist's progress grid: rows are videos, columns are members. Green = complete, partial fill = in progress, grey = not started. This makes accountability visible at a glance.

XP & Levels

You earn XP (experience points) for learning actions across the app:

ActionXP reward

Maintaining a daily streak+10 XP per day
Unlocking an achievement+25–100 XP (varies)
Hosting a watch party+20 XP

Your current level and XP bar appear on your profile and beside your name on the leaderboard. Leveling up requires progressively more XP — each level threshold is higher than the last.

Streaks

A streak is the number of consecutive days you've watched at least one video. Streaks reset at midnight in your local time zone.

TermDefinition
Current streakConsecutive days with at least one video watched or completed, counting backward from today.
Best streakYour longest consecutive run ever.
Streak resetOccurs when a full calendar day passes without any video activity. The streak goes to 0 and rebuilds from the next active day.
Important
Streaks are based on your local browser clock. If you travel across time zones, activity is attributed to your device's local midnight. There is currently no manual day-credit feature — watch at least one video each calendar day to keep your streak alive.

Notifications

Click the bell icon in the navbar to open the Notification Center. Notifications arrive in real time via Socket.io — no page refresh needed. Five categories:

CategoryWhen it fires
AchievementWhen you unlock a badge
SocialGroup invites, watch party invitations
ReminderA scheduled video is due today

Mark individual notifications read by clicking them, or use Clear all to dismiss everything at once.

Profile & settings

Your profile

Your profile at /profile shows your level badge, XP bar, total watch time, videos completed, current streak, and achievement showcase. If public profiles are enabled, your page is accessible at /profile/:username and can be linked in your portfolio or resume.

Theme

Toggle between dark and light mode using the sun/moon icon in the navbar. Your preference is saved to your account and applies immediately.

Password

Change your password from the Profile page. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt — they are never stored in plain text.

Admin panel

This section is for users with the admin role only.

Feature flags

The Admin Command Center at /admin gives global on/off control over 18 features grouped into five categories:

GroupFlags
AIAI chat, brainstorm, quiz, flashcards
StudyNotes, heatmap, Focus Lock, spaced repetition, Pomodoro timer
SocialWatch party, groups, social feed, public profiles
GamificationAchievements, leaderboard, streaks
ContentCustom playlists, playlist sync

Turning off a flag hides the feature globally for all non-admin users. Enabling maintenance_mode blocks all non-admin access with a maintenance screen.

Per-user overrides

Search any user from the admin panel. For each feature flag you can set a 3-way toggle:

  • FORCED ON — always enabled for that user regardless of the global setting. Useful for beta access.
  • GLOBAL — follows the global flag (default).
  • FORCED OFF — always disabled for that user. Useful for support debugging or disciplinary restrictions.

Impersonation (Shadow Mode)

Click "Impersonate" on any user in the Users section to browse the site exactly as that user sees it. A red banner is shown to you while in shadow mode — it is invisible to the impersonated user. All shadow-mode actions are written to the audit log. Click "Exit Shadow" to return to your admin account.

Troubleshooting

Video won't load

Try refreshing — YouTube embeds occasionally time out on first load. If a playlist is private, it cannot be imported. PlanTube requires public or unlisted playlists.

AI tools return no response

The video may not have auto-generated captions on YouTube. Manually-uploaded caption files are not supported. Check that the AI feature is enabled — an admin may have turned it off globally.

Watch party guests can't join

Make sure the host created the room first (it only exists while the host has Focus Mode open). Codes are case-insensitive. If the preview card shows a different video than expected, clicking "Go & Join" takes the guest to the correct video automatically.

Note
Rooms are stored in server memory — not in a database. If the server restarts, all active rooms are cleared. Guests will receive a "Room not found" error and the host will need to create a new room.

Progress not saving

Watch position syncs to the server every 10 seconds while playing. Closing the tab immediately after pausing may lose the last few seconds. Make sure you're logged in — guest sessions are not synced.

Notifications not appearing

If you've been on the same page for a long time, refresh to re-establish the socket connection. The bell icon shows a pulsing indicator while the connection is live.
